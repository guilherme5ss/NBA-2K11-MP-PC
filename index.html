    NBA 2K11
    My Player Mode Post-Game Press Conference FAQ

    Version 0.4. For NBA 2K11, English-language, PC (applies to other platforms). By Mister Mouth

    1. Purpose

    Following almost all regular- and post-season NBA games in NBA 2K11's My Player mode, your player is asked a question by a member of the in-game press, to which he can respond with one of four qualities: Professionalism, Arrogance, Loyalty, or Indifference. Your choice of response can affect three ratings attached your player: Teammate Chemistry, League-Wide Popularity, and Local Fan Support. These ratings in turn can change the play of the game for your player. For example, a player with low Teammate Chemistry will find the ball passed to him less often.

    The purpose of this FAQ is to provide a list of the questions that can be posed to your player and the personal ratings changes that result from each answer. This will allow you to optimize your created ballers' personal ratings.

    2. How to Read

    In the "Questions" section is a list of questions, sorted alphabetically - note that leading articles are not dropped for the purpose of sorting. On the nextline after each question is a tab-delineated list with entries "P:", "A:", "L:", and "I:" respectively for the answer with the qualities Professionalism, Arrogance, Loyalty, and Indifference. Following the colon for each answer quality is a list of three symbols from the set "+", "=", "-", and "?". The first slot records how answering with the given question with the given quality changes the Teammate Chemistry, the second the League-Wide Popularity, and the third the Local Fan Support. A "+" indicates an increase; an "=", no change; "-", a decrease; and a "?", that the result is unknown.

    For example, consider the following (fictitious) entry:

    My sources in the league office indicate that a management change may be in the works. How do you feel about your coaches?

    P:=-+ A:-+- L:??? I:???

    This indicates that, if an in-game reporter asks your player the given question ("My sources in the league office indicate...",) and you answer with Professionalism, the result will be that Teammate Chemistry will remain unchanged, League-Wide Popularity will decrease, and Local Fan Support will increase. If, on the other hand, you answer with Arrogance, Teammate Chemistry will decrease, League-Wide Popularity will increase, and Local Fan Support will decrease. The results from answering with Loyalty or Indifference are unknown, so you can help build this FAQ by choosing one of those answers and e-mailing me the results. To do that, see the section on "How To Contribute/Correct" below!

    A caveat may prove useful. The texts of questions in this FAQ are (as close as possible) the _written texts_ of questions from the PC version of the game. For some questions, the audio differs from the written text. So make sure to compare the written text of the question you're dealing with to the text in this FAQ, and be aware that some variance may exist if you're playing on a platform other than PC.

    4. How to Contribute/Correct

    If you want to contribute to or correct this FAQ, please contact me at imacdcptn@yahoo.com. You can send contributions or corrections in the form of questions and answer results in the above format.

    To aid your efforts, I'll share a trick that will allow you to find the answers many questions quickly. First, unless you've already done so, turn Autosave off: in My Player mode, load the options menu, then go to "NBA> Options>Game Mode Options" and set "Autosave" to "Off" - save the game manually after disabling Autosave, or the change to the Autosave setting won't be saved! Play a game through as normal until late in the 4th quarter. Save the game and exit, "Pause Menu>Quit>Save and Exit Game." Make sure you choose a file to which to save your game after you exit, as NBA 2K11 will no longer autosave the game to the file you were previously using! Also, it's not a good idea to overwrite your main save file with an in-game save, since there are some bugs with in-game saving that can result in corrupt save files; the best-known of these will ruin your save if you save right before or during a time out, so don't do that. Now, load the in-game save. You'll find yourself back in game at the last break in play. Play through to the end and answer the press conference question as usual, noting the question and results from your answer choice. Then, repeat the last two steps - quit My Player, re-load the in-game save, and play to the end and answer a question again, noting the question and result. Questions are determined by significant game/season results, so repeatedly replaying the end of a given game will almost always result in the asking of the same question, or sometimes members of a small set of basically equivalent questions. So, using this trick, you can directly discover the results from all the answers to a question or group of similar questions.
